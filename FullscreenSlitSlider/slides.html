Rainbow coffee

The rainbow coffee is a healthy alternative, created by Too Many Chiefs, a cafe in Brighton in Melbourne’s south east, and owners claim the drink gives you your vitamin C fix, fights the flu, boosts your immune system and acts as an anti-inflammatory.

www.toomanychiefs.org

Avalanche

Avalanche is one of the items from the menu at Milkcow. It is made by dusted espresso beans, topped with fary floss. The amazing and experimental part of this type of coffee is when you pour the espresso over the fairy floss.

www.milkcow.com.au

Caviar Coffee

The brill’ brainchild of Lavazza and Adrià himself, coffee caviar is created using the molecular gastronomic technique 'spherification' (don’t try it at home). Served on a delicate spoon and propped onto some sweet velvety cream.

www.Lavazza.com

Mushroom Coffee

Matcha Mylkbar opened in St Kilda last week, releasing what we can pretty safely declare is the first mushroom latte in the world. The mushroom lattes are made with chaga mushroom, vanilla spiced almond mylk and a side of agave, it's a taste sensation.

www.matchamylkbar.com

Tumeric Latte

It is a drink fit for Midas, with an ochre colour so vivid it doesn’t need an Instagram filter. It’s not made of gold, but it might as well be, given its cult following. “Golden milk” or turmeric latte – an unlikely combination of nut milk and juiced turmeric root.

www.talltimbercafe.com.au
